# BioVault: Sovereign Health Data Infrastructure

## The Problem: Healthcare Data is Broken

**250,000 Americans die annually** from medical errors rooted in fragmented records (Johns Hopkins, BMJ 2016).

**$500 billion per year** wasted on administrative overhead (JAMA 2020).

**Healthcare leads all industries in data breaches** for the 14th consecutive year, with an average cost of nearly **$10 million per breach**. In the US alone, approximately **200 million individuals** have had their health records potentially compromised.

**Patients cannot access their own complete histories, control who sees their data, or move records between providers.**

**This is not a bug. It is the architecture.**

Legacy Electronic Medical Record (EMR) systems centralize control in institutions, fragment data across silos, treat consent as theater, and lock patients into vendor ecosystems.

The result: preventable deaths, staggering waste, catastrophic breaches, and zero patient sovereignty.

---

## The Solution: BioVault

**BioVault** is a sovereign, collaborative EMR system that replaces institutional control with **patient ownership**. Built on:

- **Distributed ledgers** for immutable audit trails
- **End-to-end encryption** with client-side key management
- **Open standards** (FHIR, DICOM, LOINC, SNOMED)
- **Verifiable credentials** for trust without bureaucracy

Patients control access through **granular, revocable consent**. Every access produces an **immutable audit receipt**. Records are **portable across borders and systems**. Emergency responders unlock minimal datasets with **justification and accountability**.

## Why Now

**Technology is ready:**

Modern distributed ledgers can process over 100,000+ transactions per second with sub-second finality, while encryption standards such as AES-256, Curve25519, and post-quantum–ready schemes are mature and globally audited.

**Regulatory momentum:**

Frameworks including GDPR (EU), HIPAA (US), and ISO/IEC 27001 & 27701 now mandate verifiable access control, immutable auditability, and patient-centered data governance.

Sovereignty is no longer an aspiration — it is becoming a regulatory baseline.

**Market opportunity:**

The global healthcare information technology (Health IT) sector was valued at approximately $430 billion in 2023, and is projected to exceed $1.5 trillion by 2030, growing at an estimated 15% CAGR (Grand View Research, Deloitte Global Health Outlook 2024).

Within that sector, the Electronic Medical Records (EMR) market alone accounted for roughly $34 billion in 2023, with projections surpassing $85 billion by 2030 (MarketsandMarkets, Fortune Business Insights, 2024).

This expansion represents an unprecedented reinvestment in digital health infrastructure — yet current EMR systems remain institution-centric, fragmented, and opaque, leaving patients without real control over their own information.

**Moral imperative:**

Access to one's own medical data is a fundamental right.

BioVault exists to ensure that digital health innovation strengthens autonomy, privacy, and trust — not surveillance or institutional dependency.
